About Risk Management

The Risk Management department protects Wiley University's assets, personnel, and operations through comprehensive risk assessment, insurance management, and safety compliance programs. We work proactively to identify, evaluate, and mitigate potential risks across all university activities and facilities.

Our services include coordinating the university's insurance programs, processing workers' compensation claims, reviewing contracts for liability exposure, and ensuring compliance with safety regulations. We partner with departments across campus to develop risk mitigation strategies and provide guidance on event liability, vehicle use policies, and incident reporting procedures.

Our Services

Insurance Programs

We coordinate the university's property, general liability, and auto insurance programs. Contact us for certificate of insurance requests or coverage questions.

Incident Reporting

All campus incidents and accidents must be reported promptly using our Incident Report Form. Timely reporting ensures proper documentation and follow-up.

Workers' Compensation

Employees who sustain work-related injuries should report within 24 hours and complete a Workers' Compensation Claim form. We coordinate with approved medical providers.

Contract Review

Submit contracts for risk and liability assessment before signing. Standard reviews take 5-7 business days. Do not execute agreements without Risk Management approval.

Vehicle Use & Fleet

Drivers must complete a Driver Authorization Application and pass an MVR check. Authorization is valid for one year. See Transportation & Fleet for vehicle reservations.

Event Liability

Events with external vendors, high-risk activities, or alcohol service require liability review. Submit event details at least 2 weeks in advance for waivers and coverage guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Report all work-related injuries within 24 hours by completing a Workers' Compensation Claim form. Notify your supervisor immediately. For emergencies, seek medical attention first, then report. You may visit any authorized medical provider; contact our office for the approved provider list.

Submit an Insurance Certificate Request form with the third party's name, address, and any specific coverage requirements. Standard requests are processed within 3-5 business days. Rush requests may be accommodated for time-sensitive situations. Include the contract or agreement requiring the certificate.

Drivers must be at least 21 years old, have a valid driver's license, complete a Driver Authorization Application, and pass a Motor Vehicle Record (MVR) check. Authorization is valid for one year and must be renewed. Drivers with major violations in the past 3 years may be ineligible.

Events involving external vendors, alcohol service, high-risk activities, or large gatherings typically require additional liability coverage or waivers. Submit event details to Risk Management for review at least 2 weeks in advance. We'll advise on insurance requirements and provide liability waiver templates if needed.

Standard contract reviews take 5-7 business days. Complex contracts with significant liability exposure may require additional time. Submit contracts well before your deadline and indicate any time constraints. Do not sign contracts before Risk Management review is complete.
