About Transportation & Fleet Management

All persons who park or have a vehicle on Wiley University's campus are required to have a permit. Students and employees must register their vehicle(s) to park on campus. Permits are available by completing the permit request online at wileyc.edu/mvr-application or by completing an application at the Campus Police Department located on the 1st floor of McLeod Hall.

Permits are FREE but must be properly displayed for a vehicle to be legally parked on campus. The campus speed limit is 20 mph. Parking regulations are enforced 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. Contact Campus Police at (903) 930-1637 for parking questions or to report violations.

Our Services

Parking Services

Students receive parking stickers (non-transferable). Faculty/staff receive hangtags that can move between vehicles. Register online or at Campus Police in McLeod Hall.

Shuttle Services

Campus shuttle service operates during the academic year. Check the campus app or website for current routes and schedules. Free for students, faculty, and staff with valid ID.

Fleet Vehicles

University vehicles require approved Driver Authorization with MVR check. Submit reservation requests at least 5 business days in advance. Complete pre/post-trip inspections required.

Citation Appeals

Appeals are two-tiered: initial review by Chief of Police, then Appeals Committee if needed. Submit appeals within 10 days. Pay citations at Business Office in King Administration Building.

Accessible Parking

ADA accessible spaces require a valid state-issued placard or license plate. Parking in ADA spaces without proper credentials results in a $50 fine. Report violations to Campus Police.

Visitor Parking

Visitors must check in at any guard station. Park in the visitor lot at Wiley Avenue and University intersection. Requires driver's license and insurance. Contact your campus host for assistance.

Parking Permits

Permits are FREE! All vehicles must display a valid permit. Register online at wileyc.edu/mvr-application or at Campus Police in McLeod Hall.

Frequently Asked Questions

Register online through the MyWiley Portal or submit a Parking Permit Application. You'll need your vehicle information (make, model, color, license plate, and state). Permits can be picked up at our office or mailed to your campus address. All vehicles must be registered within the first week of classes.

Student commuter permits are valid in designated student lots only. Resident students park in residence hall lots. Parking in faculty/staff lots, visitor spaces, or restricted areas will result in a citation. See the Campus Parking Map for designated zones. Permits must be displayed at all times.

Submit a Citation Appeal Form within 10 business days of receiving the citation. Include any supporting documentation (photos, proof of permit, etc.). Appeals are reviewed by the Parking Appeals Committee, and you'll receive a decision via email within 2 weeks. Unpaid citations will result in a hold on your account.

Submit a Vehicle Reservation Request at least 5 business days in advance. You must be an authorized driver (completed Driver Authorization Application with approved MVR check). Include your trip details, destination, and number of passengers. Vehicles are assigned based on availability and trip needs.

Campus shuttle service operates Monday through Friday during the academic year. Routes and schedules vary; check the current schedule on our website or the campus app. Service is reduced during breaks and summer sessions. The shuttle is free for all students, faculty, and staff with valid ID.
