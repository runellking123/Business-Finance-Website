About Financial Aid

The Office of Financial Aid at Wiley University is committed to making quality education accessible and affordable for all students. We administer federal, state, and institutional aid programs including grants, scholarships, loans, and work-study opportunities. Our team works closely with students and families to navigate the financial aid process and develop funding strategies that support academic success.

Our counselors provide personalized guidance throughout your educational journey, from completing the FAFSA to understanding your award letter and maintaining eligibility. We encourage students to complete the FAFSA as soon after October 1 as possible to maximize eligibility for programs with limited funds. Visit us in the Willis J King Administration Building, Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

Types of Financial Aid

Grants

Federal and state grants, including the Federal Pell Grant and FSEOG, provide funding that does not need to be repaid. Eligibility is based on financial need as determined by your FAFSA.

Scholarships

Wiley University offers institutional, merit-based, and need-based scholarships. Additional external scholarships are available through various organizations and foundations.

Federal Loans

Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans help cover educational expenses. Parent PLUS Loans are available for dependent students. Loans must be repaid with interest.

Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)

Maintaining satisfactory academic progress is the responsibility of each student and is required to remain eligible for federal financial aid. In accordance with the Higher Education Act of 1965 and federal regulations, Wiley University reviews each student's progress at the end of each academic year (August 1 through May 31). Students are evaluated both qualitatively (GPA) and quantitatively (completion rate and maximum timeframe). The same SAP standards apply to all students regardless of whether they receive financial aid. Students who fail to meet SAP requirements may appeal by submitting an SAP Appeal Form with documentation of extenuating circumstances.

Frequently Asked Questions

Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) at studentaid.gov. Use Wiley University's school code when applying. After submitting your FAFSA, allow 2-3 weeks for processing. Check your MyWiley Portal for any additional documents we may need.

Our priority deadline is March 1st for the upcoming academic year. Students who submit the FAFSA by this date receive first consideration for limited funds like work-study and certain grants. You can still apply after the deadline, but some aid may no longer be available.

Award letters are typically sent 2-4 weeks after your FAFSA is processed and any required verification is complete. Check your MyWiley Portal regularly for your award status. You must accept or decline your awards before aid can be disbursed to your account.

If you lose eligibility due to Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP), you may submit an SAP Appeal Form explaining the circumstances and your plan for improvement. If approved, you'll be placed on a probationary status. Meet with a financial aid counselor to discuss your options.

Submit the Scholarship Application form available on this page. Many institutional scholarships use this single application. The deadline is typically April 15th for the following academic year. Check our website for additional external scholarship opportunities throughout the year.
