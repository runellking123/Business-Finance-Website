1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines and procedures for travel conducted on behalf of Wiley University. The purpose is to ensure that university-related travel is conducted in a fiscally responsible manner, complies with applicable regulations, and supports the mission of the institution while providing fair and reasonable reimbursement to travelers.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources; 2 CFR 200.474 - Travel Costs)

2. Scope

This policy applies to all travel conducted on behalf of the University by:

  • Full-time and part-time employees
  • Board of Trustees members
  • Students traveling for university-sponsored activities
  • Guests and consultants when travel is paid by the University
  • Prospective employees during interviews

This policy covers travel funded by all university sources, including operating funds, grants, contracts, and auxiliary funds.

3. Definitions

Official Travel: Travel required for the performance of university business or attendance at professional activities approved by appropriate authorities.

Travel Authorization: Prior approval required before incurring travel expenses.

Per Diem: A daily allowance for meals and incidental expenses during travel.

Reimbursable Expenses: Actual, reasonable, and necessary costs incurred during official travel.

Travel Advance: Funds provided to a traveler before a trip to cover anticipated expenses.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University supports travel that advances the institution's mission, including professional development, student recruitment, research, athletic competition, and institutional representation. All travel must be pre-approved, cost-effective, and properly documented.

4.1 Travel Authorization

  • All travel must be approved in advance using the Travel Authorization Form
  • Out-of-state travel requires approval from the Division Head
  • International travel requires approval from the President
  • Travel using grant funds must comply with sponsor requirements
  • Authorization must include estimated costs and funding source

4.2 Approval Authority

In-state travel (overnight) Department Head

5. Transportation

5.1 Air Travel

  • Coach/economy class is the standard for all air travel
  • Business class may be approved for international flights over 6 hours with SVP approval
  • Travelers should book at least 14 days in advance when possible
  • Lowest logical airfare must be selected (within 2 hours of preferred times)
  • Frequent flyer benefits are the property of the traveler

5.2 Ground Transportation

  • University vehicles should be used when available and practical
  • Personal vehicle mileage reimbursed at current IRS rate
  • Rental cars require prior approval and must include liability coverage
  • Standard or intermediate-size vehicles unless group size requires larger
  • Taxi, rideshare, and public transit are reimbursable with receipts

5.3 Mileage Reimbursement

Personal vehicle use is reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage rate. Mileage is calculated from the employee's primary work location or residence, whichever is less. Commuting mileage is not reimbursable.

6. Lodging

  • Standard single-occupancy rooms at moderately priced hotels
  • Government or educational rates should be requested when available
  • Conference hotels may be used even if rates exceed standard limits
  • Lodging with family or friends: $30 per night flat rate in lieu of receipt
  • Actual lodging costs reimbursed with itemized receipt
  • No reimbursement for room upgrades, premium channels, or minibar

7. Meals and Per Diem

7.1 Per Diem Rates

Meal Rate
Breakfast $15.00
Lunch $20.00
Dinner $30.00
Daily Total $65.00

Per diem rates for high-cost areas may be adjusted based on GSA rates with prior approval.

7.2 Meal Eligibility

  • Breakfast: Departure before 6:30 AM or overnight travel
  • Lunch: Travel extending over normal lunch period (11:00 AM - 2:00 PM)
  • Dinner: Return after 7:00 PM or overnight travel
  • Meals provided at conferences or included in registration must be deducted

8. Other Reimbursable Expenses

  • Conference and seminar registration fees
  • Parking and toll fees with receipts
  • Baggage fees (one checked bag)
  • Business-related phone calls and internet access
  • Tips for baggage handling (reasonable amounts)
  • Visa and passport fees for required international travel

9. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

  • Personal entertainment and recreation
  • Alcoholic beverages
  • Traffic violations and parking tickets
  • Personal items (toiletries, clothing, etc.)
  • Travel insurance (beyond rental car coverage)
  • Expenses for accompanying family members
  • Laundry/dry cleaning (unless trip exceeds 5 days)
  • Upgrades to travel accommodations

10. Travel Advances

  • Advances available for travel expected to exceed $200
  • Request must be submitted at least 10 business days before travel
  • Maximum advance is 80% of estimated reimbursable expenses
  • Advances must be reconciled within 10 business days of return
  • Outstanding advances may be deducted from payroll

11. Travel Expense Reports

  • Submit within 10 business days of completing travel
  • Include all original receipts for expenses over $25
  • Itemized receipts required for lodging and registration
  • Credit card statements are not acceptable as receipts
  • Lost receipts require signed lost receipt affidavit
  • Supervisor approval required before submission to Business Office

12. Grant-Funded Travel

Travel charged to federal grants must comply with 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance) and sponsor-specific requirements, including:

  • Fly America Act requirements for international air travel
  • Prior sponsor approval when required
  • Documentation of travel's purpose and benefit to the project
  • More restrictive sponsor policies supersede this policy

(Reference: 2 CFR 200.474 - Travel costs must be reasonable and consistent with institutional policy)

13. Compliance and Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:

  • Denial of reimbursement
  • Personal liability for unauthorized expenses
  • Revocation of travel privileges
  • Disciplinary action

14. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and a demonstrated, stable financial base to support its mission.
  • Standard 13.6 (Federal and State Responsibilities): The institution complies with applicable federal and state requirements for the proper management and administration of financial resources.
