1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for managing liability associated with events held on Wiley University property or sponsored by the University at off-campus locations. The purpose is to protect the University, its employees, students, and guests from potential liability arising from event-related activities while ensuring appropriate insurance coverage and risk mitigation measures are in place.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources; Standard 13.8 - Institutional Environment)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All events held on university property, regardless of sponsor
  • University-sponsored events held at off-campus locations
  • Events hosted by registered student organizations
  • Events hosted by external groups using university facilities
  • Academic and athletic events
  • Community outreach and recruitment events
  • Camps, conferences, and workshops

3. Definitions

University-Sponsored Event: An event officially organized, funded, or endorsed by the University, a university department, or a registered student organization.

External Event: An event organized by a non-university entity using university facilities.

High-Risk Event: An event with elevated potential for injury, property damage, or liability claims.

Certificate of Insurance (COI): A document proving an organization has liability insurance coverage.

Indemnification Agreement: A contract in which one party agrees to hold another harmless from liability.

Waiver and Release: A document in which participants acknowledge risks and release the University from liability.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University supports a vibrant campus life that includes various events and activities. The University requires appropriate risk management measures for all events to protect participants, guests, and university assets. Event organizers must comply with this policy and obtain necessary approvals, insurance, and documentation before events take place.

4.1 Event Classification

Risk Level Event Types Requirements
Low Risk Meetings, lectures, receptions, career fairs, informational sessions Facility reservation; standard event registration
Medium Risk Sports tournaments, concerts, outdoor festivals, events with food service Event registration; may require additional insurance or waivers
High Risk Events with alcohol, physical activities, minors, large crowds, inflatables, animals Risk Management review; insurance certificates; waivers; additional approvals

5. High-Risk Activities

The following activities are considered high-risk and require Risk Management approval:

  • Events serving alcohol
  • Sporting events and athletic competitions
  • Events involving physical activities (obstacle courses, climbing walls, etc.)
  • Events with inflatables, carnival rides, or amusement devices
  • Events involving fireworks or pyrotechnics
  • Events with animals (petting zoos, service animal demonstrations)
  • Events involving minors (camps, K-12 visits)
  • Water-based activities
  • Events with more than 500 attendees
  • Events at off-campus locations
  • Events with hazardous materials or demonstrations

6. Insurance Requirements

6.1 External Organizations

External organizations using university facilities must provide:

  • Certificate of Insurance (COI) at least 14 days before the event
  • General liability coverage: minimum $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate
  • Wiley University named as additional insured
  • 30-day notice of cancellation provision
  • Coverage for the specific event dates and activities

6.2 Vendor and Contractor Insurance

Vendors providing services at events must carry:

  • General liability: $1,000,000 per occurrence
  • Workers' compensation as required by law
  • Auto liability if vehicles are used: $1,000,000 combined single limit
  • Liquor liability if serving alcohol: $1,000,000
  • Additional coverage as determined by Risk Management

6.3 Special Event Insurance

Some events may require special event insurance policies:

  • Event cancellation insurance for major events
  • Participant accident insurance for athletic events
  • Equipment rental insurance

7. Waivers and Releases

7.1 When Required

Waivers and releases are required for:

  • All high-risk activities
  • Athletic and recreational activities
  • Activities involving physical exertion or risk of injury
  • Field trips and off-campus activities
  • Volunteer activities

7.2 Waiver Requirements

  • Waivers must be signed before participation
  • Use university-approved waiver forms
  • For minors, parent/guardian signature required
  • Waivers retained by sponsoring department for 7 years
  • Risk Management may require additional language for specific activities

8. Events with Minors

Events involving participants under 18 years of age require:

  • Compliance with the University's Protection of Minors Policy
  • Background checks for all adults with direct contact
  • Parent/guardian consent and emergency contact forms
  • Appropriate supervision ratios
  • Medical authorization forms
  • Incident reporting procedures

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.8 requires a safe and secure institutional environment)

9. Events with Alcohol

Events where alcohol will be served must:

  • Receive approval from the SVP for Business & Finance
  • Comply with the University Alcohol Policy
  • Use a licensed, insured caterer or vendor for service
  • Provide proof of liquor liability insurance ($1,000,000 minimum)
  • Ensure proper age verification and control measures
  • Have security personnel present if more than 100 attendees
  • Provide non-alcoholic beverage and food alternatives

10. Facility Use Agreements

External organizations must sign a Facility Use Agreement that includes:

  • Indemnification and hold harmless provisions
  • Insurance requirements
  • Rules and regulations for facility use
  • Cancellation and refund terms
  • Damage and liability provisions
  • Security and safety requirements

11. Incident Reporting

All incidents occurring during events must be reported:

  • Injuries requiring medical attention: immediately to Campus Security
  • Property damage: within 24 hours to Facilities Management
  • All incidents: Incident Report Form to Risk Management within 48 hours
  • Potential liability claims: immediately to Risk Management

12. Responsibilities

12.1 Event Organizer

  • Complete event registration and approval process
  • Identify and communicate event risks
  • Obtain necessary insurance and documentation
  • Collect waivers from participants
  • Ensure compliance with all applicable policies
  • Report incidents to appropriate offices

12.2 Risk Management

  • Review high-risk events for approval
  • Verify insurance certificates and coverage
  • Provide guidance on risk mitigation
  • Maintain approved waiver forms
  • Manage claims arising from events

12.3 Sponsoring Department

  • Ensure event organizers understand and comply with this policy
  • Approve departmental events
  • Maintain event documentation
  • Provide supervision and oversight

13. Compliance and Review

Events that do not comply with this policy may be cancelled or denied facility access. Repeated non-compliance may result in loss of event privileges. This policy is reviewed annually by Risk Management in consultation with Student Affairs, Facilities Management, and legal counsel.

14. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and properly manages its liabilities.
  • Standard 13.7 (Physical Resources): The institution maintains physical facilities, including those used for events, that are adequate and safe.
  • Standard 13.8 (Institutional Environment): The institution provides a safe and secure environment for students, faculty, staff, and visitors.
