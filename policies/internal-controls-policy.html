1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for internal controls at Wiley University to ensure the integrity of financial reporting, compliance with applicable laws and regulations, and the effectiveness and efficiency of operations. A sound system of internal controls is essential for safeguarding university assets, preventing and detecting fraud, and maintaining public trust.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources; Standard 13.6 - Federal and State Responsibilities)

2. Scope

This policy applies to all university employees, departments, and operations involving financial transactions, asset management, and compliance activities. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • All financial transactions and reporting
  • Cash handling and management
  • Procurement and purchasing activities
  • Payroll processing
  • Grant and contract administration
  • Asset acquisition and disposal
  • Information systems with financial data

3. Definitions

Internal Controls: Processes designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives in effectiveness and efficiency of operations, reliability of financial reporting, and compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

Segregation of Duties: The division of responsibilities among different individuals to reduce the risk of error or fraud.

Reconciliation: The process of comparing two sets of records to verify accuracy and identify discrepancies.

Authorization: The approval of transactions by personnel with delegated authority.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University is committed to maintaining a comprehensive system of internal controls that adheres to the standards established by the Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission (COSO) framework. The University's internal control system encompasses five interrelated components:

4.1 Control Environment

The control environment sets the tone of the organization and influences the control consciousness of its people. Key elements include:

  • Commitment to integrity and ethical values at all levels
  • Board of Trustees oversight responsibility
  • Organizational structure with clear lines of authority
  • Commitment to competence through hiring and training practices
  • Accountability for internal control responsibilities

4.2 Risk Assessment

The University shall identify and analyze risks relevant to the achievement of its objectives. This includes:

  • Annual risk assessment conducted by the Business Office
  • Identification of fraud risk factors
  • Analysis of changes that could significantly impact the control system
  • Documentation of identified risks and mitigation strategies

4.3 Control Activities

Control activities are the policies and procedures that help ensure management directives are carried out:

  • Segregation of Duties: No single individual shall have control over all phases of a transaction (authorization, custody, recording, reconciliation)
  • Authorization and Approval: All transactions must be authorized by personnel with appropriate delegated authority
  • Verification and Reconciliation: Account reconciliations must be performed monthly and reviewed by supervisors
  • Physical Controls: Adequate safeguards for assets, including restricted access and periodic physical inventories
  • Documentation: Adequate documentation and records must be maintained for all transactions

4.4 Information and Communication

Relevant information must be identified, captured, and communicated in a timely manner:

  • Financial information systems shall provide accurate and timely data
  • Policies and procedures shall be documented and accessible
  • Channels for reporting suspected fraud or irregularities shall be established
  • External reporting requirements shall be met accurately and timely

4.5 Monitoring

The internal control system must be monitored to assess effectiveness:

  • Ongoing monitoring through regular management and supervisory activities
  • Periodic evaluations by internal audit
  • Timely correction of identified deficiencies
  • Annual assessment of internal control effectiveness

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 requires demonstration of financial stability and effective resource management)

5. Procedures and Responsibilities

5.1 Board of Trustees

  • Provides oversight of the internal control system
  • Reviews annual financial statements and audit reports
  • Ensures management establishes and maintains adequate internal controls

5.2 Senior Vice President for Business & Finance

  • Establishes and maintains the internal control framework
  • Ensures compliance with this policy across all departments
  • Reports internal control matters to the Board of Trustees
  • Coordinates with external auditors

5.3 Controller

  • Implements internal control procedures in financial operations
  • Conducts periodic reviews of internal control effectiveness
  • Ensures timely reconciliation of accounts
  • Maintains documentation of internal control procedures

5.4 Department Heads

  • Implement internal controls within their areas of responsibility
  • Ensure staff understand and follow control procedures
  • Report suspected irregularities to the Business Office
  • Participate in risk assessments as requested

5.5 All Employees

  • Comply with established internal control procedures
  • Report suspected fraud, waste, or abuse
  • Protect university assets in their custody
  • Maintain accurate records of transactions

6. Specific Control Requirements

6.1 Cash Handling

  • Cash receipts must be deposited within one business day
  • Cash handling duties must be segregated
  • Cash drawers must be reconciled daily
  • Access to cash must be restricted to authorized personnel

6.2 Purchasing and Disbursements

  • Purchase orders required for purchases over $500
  • Three-way match required (PO, receiving report, invoice) before payment
  • Check signing authority limited to designated personnel
  • Dual signatures required for checks over $10,000

6.3 Payroll

  • Segregation between personnel records, timekeeping, and payroll processing
  • Supervisor approval required for all time records
  • Periodic reconciliation of payroll records to HR records
  • Review of payroll register by independent personnel before processing

7. Reporting Fraud or Irregularities

Employees who suspect fraud, waste, or abuse should report concerns through one of the following channels:

  • Direct supervisor (unless involved in the suspected activity)
  • Business Office
  • Human Resources
  • Anonymous reporting hotline

Retaliation against employees who report suspected irregularities in good faith is strictly prohibited.

8. Compliance and Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually by the Senior Vice President for Business & Finance and updated as necessary to reflect changes in regulations, organizational structure, or best practices. Compliance with this policy is mandatory, and violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.6 requires compliance with federal and state requirements for financial accountability)

9. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and a demonstrated, stable financial base to support its mission.
  • Standard 13.6 (Federal and State Responsibilities): The institution complies with applicable federal and state requirements for the proper management and administration of financial resources.
  • Standard 7.1 (Institutional Planning): The institution engages in ongoing, comprehensive, and integrated research-based planning and evaluation processes.
