1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the acquisition, maintenance, operation, and disposal of university-owned vehicles. The purpose is to ensure safe, efficient, and cost-effective management of the university fleet while minimizing liability and environmental impact.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources; Standard 13.7 - Physical Resources)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All university-owned motor vehicles
  • Leased vehicles under university agreements
  • Golf carts and utility vehicles
  • All departments and individuals operating university vehicles
  • Vehicle maintenance and repair activities

3. Definitions

Fleet Vehicle: Any motor vehicle owned or leased by Wiley University.

Pool Vehicle: A shared vehicle available to multiple departments for temporary use.

Assigned Vehicle: A vehicle designated for use by a specific department or individual.

Fleet Manager: The individual responsible for coordinating fleet operations.

Preventive Maintenance: Scheduled maintenance activities to prevent vehicle breakdowns.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University maintains a fleet of vehicles to support institutional operations. Fleet vehicles must be acquired, maintained, and operated in a manner that ensures safety, cost-effectiveness, and compliance with all applicable regulations. Personal use of fleet vehicles is prohibited.

5. Vehicle Acquisition

5.1 Acquisition Process

  • All vehicle acquisitions require approval from SVP for Business & Finance
  • Request must include justification, cost analysis, and funding source
  • Follow university Purchasing Policy for procurement
  • Consider total cost of ownership (purchase price, fuel, maintenance, insurance)
  • Preference for fuel-efficient and environmentally friendly vehicles

5.3 Leasing vs. Purchasing

The decision to lease or purchase should consider:

  • Expected useful life and mileage
  • Total cost of ownership comparison
  • Maintenance and warranty coverage
  • Budget availability and funding restrictions
  • Flexibility needs

6. Vehicle Assignment

6.1 Pool Vehicles

  • Available for reservation by any authorized department
  • Reserved through Transportation & Fleet Services
  • Returned clean and fueled after each use
  • Usage tracked for cost allocation

6.2 Department-Assigned Vehicles

  • Assigned to departments with ongoing transportation needs
  • Department responsible for daily operations and inspections
  • Cannot be shared with other departments without approval
  • Must be available for reassignment if underutilized

6.3 Individual-Assigned Vehicles

  • Limited to positions requiring frequent vehicle use
  • Requires President or SVP approval
  • May not be taken home without written authorization
  • Subject to annual utilization review

7. Maintenance

7.1 Preventive Maintenance Schedule

Service Type Frequency
Oil change and fluid check Every 5,000 miles or 6 months
Tire rotation and inspection Every 7,500 miles
Brake inspection Every 15,000 miles
Comprehensive inspection Annually
State inspection Annually (as required)

7.2 Maintenance Responsibilities

  • Transportation & Fleet coordinates all scheduled maintenance
  • Drivers must report maintenance needs promptly
  • Complete Vehicle Condition Report before and after each trip
  • Do not operate a vehicle with known safety defects
  • Unauthorized repairs or modifications prohibited

7.3 Emergency Repairs

  • Contact Transportation & Fleet for roadside assistance
  • Emergency repairs authorized up to $200 without prior approval
  • Retain all receipts for reimbursement
  • Report all repairs to Fleet Manager upon return

8. Fuel Management

8.1 Fuel Cards

  • Fleet vehicles equipped with fuel cards
  • Cards used only for assigned vehicle
  • Purchase fuel only (no merchandise or cash advances)
  • Record odometer reading at each fill-up
  • Lost cards reported immediately

8.2 Fuel Efficiency

  • Use lowest-cost fuel grade required by vehicle
  • Monitor fuel consumption for each vehicle
  • Investigate significant changes in fuel efficiency
  • Report suspected fuel theft

9. Insurance and Liability

9.1 Insurance Coverage

  • All fleet vehicles covered under university insurance policy
  • Coverage includes liability, collision, and comprehensive
  • Authorized drivers covered while operating fleet vehicles
  • Personal vehicle use for university business requires personal insurance

9.2 Accident Reporting

  • Report all accidents immediately to Campus Security
  • Complete Accident Report Form within 24 hours
  • Do not admit fault at the scene
  • Obtain police report for accidents with injuries or significant damage
  • Forward all documentation to Risk Management

9.3 Driver Liability

Drivers may be held personally responsible for:

  • Traffic citations and fines
  • Damage due to negligence or policy violation
  • Damage during unauthorized use
  • Costs associated with DUI/DWI incidents

10. Vehicle Tracking and Records

10.1 Fleet Records

Transportation & Fleet maintains records for each vehicle including:

  • Vehicle identification and registration information
  • Acquisition date and cost
  • Maintenance and repair history
  • Fuel consumption data
  • Accident and incident reports
  • Mileage logs

10.2 GPS Tracking

  • Fleet vehicles may be equipped with GPS tracking
  • Tracking data used for route optimization and safety
  • Data may be used in accident investigations
  • Drivers will be notified if GPS tracking is installed

11. Vehicle Disposal

11.1 Disposal Criteria

Vehicles may be considered for disposal when:

  • Mileage exceeds 100,000 miles
  • Age exceeds 10 years
  • Repair costs exceed 50% of current value
  • Vehicle no longer meets operational needs
  • Safety concerns cannot be economically addressed

11.2 Disposal Methods

  • Public auction (preferred for vehicles with value)
  • Trade-in on new vehicle purchase
  • Sale to employees (at fair market value, through auction)
  • Donation to qualified organizations
  • Salvage or scrap

All disposals follow university Property Inventory Policy.

12. Environmental Considerations

  • Prefer fuel-efficient and alternative fuel vehicles when practical
  • Consider electric or hybrid vehicles for campus use
  • Proper disposal of batteries, fluids, and tires
  • Comply with all environmental regulations
  • Track and report fleet emissions as required

13. Compliance and Review

This policy is reviewed annually by Transportation & Fleet Services. Fleet utilization, costs, and efficiency are reported quarterly to the SVP for Business & Finance. Vehicles with low utilization may be reassigned or disposed of.

14. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and properly manages its assets.
  • Standard 13.7 (Physical Resources): The institution maintains physical resources, including vehicles, adequate to support its mission.
