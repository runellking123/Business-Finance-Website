1. Purpose

This policy establishes the procedures for calculating and returning Title IV federal financial aid funds when a student withdraws from Wiley University before completing 60% of the enrollment period. Federal regulations require institutions to determine the amount of Title IV aid "earned" by the student and return any "unearned" funds to the appropriate federal programs.

(Reference: 34 CFR 668.22 - Treatment of Title IV Funds When a Student Withdraws; SACSCOC Standard 12.1 - Student Financial Aid)

2. Scope

This policy applies to all students who:

  • Received or were eligible to receive Title IV federal financial aid, AND
  • Completely withdraw from all courses (officially or unofficially) before completing more than 60% of the enrollment period

Title IV programs covered by this policy include:

  • Federal Pell Grant
  • Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant (FSEOG)
  • Federal Direct Subsidized Loans
  • Federal Direct Unsubsidized Loans
  • Federal Direct PLUS Loans (Parent and Graduate)
  • Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grant
  • TEACH Grant

3. Definitions

Title IV Funds: Federal student financial aid programs authorized under Title IV of the Higher Education Act of 1965, as amended.

Withdrawal Date: The date the student began the official withdrawal process, the last date of academic attendance, or the midpoint of the term for unofficial withdrawals.

Payment Period: The semester or term for which Title IV funds were disbursed or could have been disbursed.

Earned Aid: The percentage of Title IV aid earned based on the percentage of the payment period completed before withdrawal.

Unearned Aid: Title IV funds that must be returned because the student withdrew before earning 100% of the aid.

Post-Withdrawal Disbursement: Title IV funds earned by the student but not yet disbursed at the time of withdrawal.

4. Policy Statement

When a Title IV recipient withdraws during a payment period, Wiley University must determine the amount of Title IV aid earned by the student up to that point. If the student received more aid than was earned, the unearned funds must be returned. If the student received less aid than was earned, they may be eligible for a post-withdrawal disbursement.

(Reference: 34 CFR 668.22(a) - General requirements for return of Title IV funds)

4.1 Determination of Withdrawal Date

The withdrawal date is determined as follows:

Type of Withdrawal Withdrawal Date
Official Withdrawal Date student began official withdrawal process or last date of attendance, whichever is later
Unofficial Withdrawal (stopped attending) Last documented date of academic activity
Unofficial Withdrawal (no documentation) Midpoint (50%) of the payment period
Student who does not return from approved leave Date the student was scheduled to return

4.2 Calculation of Earned Aid

The percentage of Title IV aid earned is calculated using the following formula:

Percentage Earned = Days Attended ÷ Total Days in Payment Period

(Scheduled breaks of 5+ consecutive days are excluded from total days)

If the calculated percentage is greater than 60%, the student has earned 100% of the Title IV aid for that payment period, and no return calculation is required.

4.3 Calculation of Unearned Aid

When a student withdraws before completing 60% of the payment period:

Unearned Percentage = 100% - Percentage Earned

Total Aid to Return = Unearned Percentage × Total Title IV Aid Disbursed (or that could have been disbursed)

5. Procedures

5.1 Return Calculation Process

  1. Registrar's Office notifies Financial Aid of student withdrawal and date
  2. Financial Aid determines if student received Title IV funds
  3. Financial Aid calculates percentage of payment period completed
  4. If less than 60%, Financial Aid calculates Return of Title IV Funds
  5. Financial Aid determines institutional and student portions of return
  6. Business Office processes institutional return within 45 days of withdrawal date
  7. Student notified of any balance owed to the University

5.2 Institutional Responsibility

The University must return the lesser of:

  • The unearned Title IV aid, OR
  • Institutional charges multiplied by the unearned percentage

The University must return funds within 45 days of determining the student withdrew.

5.3 Student Responsibility

The student must return:

  • Any unearned Title IV aid not covered by the institutional return
  • Unearned loan funds are repaid according to the terms of the promissory note
  • Unearned grant funds are limited to 50% of the grant amount received

6. Order of Return

Title IV funds must be returned in the following order as specified by federal regulations:

  1. Federal Direct Unsubsidized Loans
  2. Federal Direct Subsidized Loans
  3. Federal Direct PLUS Loans (Graduate Student)
  4. Federal Direct PLUS Loans (Parent)
  5. Federal Pell Grants
  6. Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grants (FSEOG)
  7. TEACH Grants
  8. Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grants

(Reference: 34 CFR 668.22(i) - Order of return of Title IV funds)

7. Post-Withdrawal Disbursements

If the student earned more aid than was disbursed prior to withdrawal, they may be eligible for a post-withdrawal disbursement:

7.1 Grant Funds

  • Disbursed within 45 days of withdrawal determination
  • Applied to current charges without student authorization
  • Credit balances refunded within 14 days

7.2 Loan Funds

  • Student (or parent for PLUS) must be notified within 30 days of withdrawal
  • Written authorization required before disbursement
  • Student has 14 days to respond to notification
  • Disbursement made within 180 days of withdrawal determination

8. Impact on Student Accounts

When Title IV funds are returned:

  • Student may owe a balance to the University for charges previously paid by returned funds
  • Student will be notified of any balance due within 30 days
  • Payment arrangements may be made through Student Accounts
  • Unpaid balances may result in registration holds and collection action

9. Special Circumstances

9.1 Modular Courses

For programs offered in modules (non-standard terms):

  • Withdrawal from one module does not trigger R2T4 if student is still enrolled in later module
  • Written confirmation of intent to enroll in later module required
  • If student does not return, R2T4 calculated based on original withdrawal date

9.2 Leave of Absence

An approved leave of absence does not trigger R2T4 if:

  • Leave does not exceed 180 days in any 12-month period
  • Student returns on the scheduled date
  • Leave is properly documented and approved

9.3 Death of Student

In the event of a student's death:

  • No return calculation is required for Title IV grant funds
  • Loan debt is discharged according to federal loan regulations

10. Student Notification

Students who withdraw are notified in writing of:

  • The amount of Title IV aid earned and unearned
  • The amount the University is required to return
  • The amount the student is required to return (if any)
  • Eligibility for post-withdrawal disbursement (if applicable)
  • Any balance due to the University
  • Timeframes for repayment

11. Examples

Example 1: A student withdraws on day 30 of a 100-day semester after receiving $5,000 in Pell Grant and $3,500 in Direct Loans.

Percentage Earned = 30 ÷ 100 = 30%
Aid Earned = 30% × $8,500 = $2,550
Aid to Return = 70% × $8,500 = $5,950

The University and student share responsibility for returning the $5,950 in unearned aid.

Example 2: A student withdraws on day 65 of a 100-day semester.

Percentage Earned = 65 ÷ 100 = 65%
Since 65% > 60%, the student has earned 100% of Title IV aid.
No return calculation is required.

12. Compliance and Review

This policy is reviewed annually to ensure compliance with 34 CFR 668.22 and other applicable federal regulations. The Financial Aid Office maintains documentation of all Return of Title IV Funds calculations and returns. These records are subject to review during federal program audits and reviews.
