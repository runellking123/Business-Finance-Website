1. Purpose

This policy establishes rules and regulations for parking and traffic control on Wiley University property. The purpose is to provide safe, orderly, and equitable access to campus parking facilities while supporting the educational mission of the institution.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.7 - Physical Resources; Texas Transportation Code)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All students, faculty, staff, and administrators
  • Visitors and guests
  • Contractors and vendors
  • All motor vehicles, including motorcycles and mopeds
  • All university-owned or controlled parking areas

3. Definitions

Permit: An official decal or hang tag authorizing parking in designated areas.

Citation: A notice of violation issued for parking or traffic infractions.

Immobilization (Boot): A device attached to a vehicle to prevent operation.

Towing: Removal of a vehicle from university property at the owner's expense.

Reserved Parking: Spaces designated for specific individuals, groups, or purposes.

4. Permit Requirements

4.1 Who Must Register

  • All students, faculty, and staff parking on campus must obtain a permit
  • Permits required Monday through Friday, 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM
  • After 5:00 PM and on weekends, permits required only in reserved spaces
  • Visitors may obtain temporary permits from Campus Security

4.2 Permit Types

Permit Type Eligible Users Authorized Areas
Faculty/Staff (Red) Full-time employees Faculty/Staff lots, general lots
Student Commuter (Blue) Commuter students Student lots only
Student Resident (Green) Residential students Residential and student lots
Reserved (Gold) Designated individuals Specific reserved spaces
Temporary Visitors, vendors Visitor lots, as designated
Motorcycle All eligible users Motorcycle parking areas

4.3 Permit Fees

Permit fees are established annually and published on the Transportation & Fleet website. Fees vary by permit type and are non-refundable except in cases of withdrawal or separation from the University.

4.4 Obtaining a Permit

  1. Complete permit application online or in person
  2. Provide valid vehicle registration information
  3. Pay applicable fees
  4. Display permit as instructed (hang tag from mirror or decal on rear window)

4.5 Permit Regulations

  • Permits are non-transferable
  • Only one vehicle may display a permit at a time
  • Lost or stolen permits must be reported immediately
  • Replacement fee charged for lost permits
  • Permits must be returned upon separation from University
  • Altered or counterfeit permits result in disciplinary action

5. Parking Regulations

5.1 General Rules

  • Park only in designated spaces appropriate for your permit type
  • Park within marked lines; do not occupy multiple spaces
  • Obey all posted signs and painted markings
  • Maximum speed limit on campus is 15 MPH
  • Yield to pedestrians at all crosswalks
  • No parking on grass, sidewalks, or fire lanes
  • Headlights must be turned off when parked

5.2 Restricted Areas

The following areas are restricted at all times:

  • Fire lanes and fire hydrant zones (within 15 feet)
  • Handicapped spaces (without valid placard)
  • Loading zones (except for active loading/unloading)
  • Reserved spaces (without appropriate permit)
  • Service and maintenance areas
  • Yellow curb zones
  • Building entrances and exits

5.3 Handicapped Parking

  • State-issued handicapped placard or license plate required
  • University permit also required
  • Violations subject to university citation and state fines

6. Violations and Fines

6.1 Fine Schedule

Violation Fine
No permit displayed $25
Parking in wrong lot/zone $25
Overtime parking (meter/time limit) $15
Improper parking (outside lines, wrong direction) $20
Fire lane violation $100
Handicapped parking violation $250
Reserved space violation $50
Blocking traffic/driveway $50
Speeding on campus $50
Failure to yield to pedestrian $50

6.2 Payment of Fines

  • Fines due within 10 business days of issuance
  • Payment accepted online, by mail, or in person
  • Late payment penalty: additional $10 after 10 days
  • Unpaid fines result in registration and transcript holds
  • For employees, unpaid fines may be deducted from payroll

6.3 Repeat Offenders

  • Three or more unpaid citations: vehicle subject to immobilization
  • Boot removal fee: $75 plus all outstanding fines
  • Five or more violations in one semester: parking privileges suspended
  • Continued violations: referral to Student Conduct or Human Resources

7. Towing

Vehicles may be towed at owner's expense for:

  • Blocking fire lanes, building access, or emergency vehicles
  • Parking in handicapped space without valid placard
  • Abandoned vehicles (unmoved for 72+ hours without permit)
  • Three or more unpaid citations on vehicle
  • Vehicles creating a safety hazard
  • During special events or emergencies as posted

To retrieve a towed vehicle, contact Campus Security for towing company information.

8. Appeals Process

8.1 Filing an Appeal

  • Appeals must be submitted within 10 business days of citation
  • Complete Citation Appeal Form online or in person
  • Provide specific grounds for appeal with any supporting documentation
  • Payment of fine not required to file appeal

8.2 Grounds for Appeal

  • Citation issued in error (incorrect vehicle or violation)
  • Permit properly displayed but not visible to officer
  • Documented emergency situation
  • Defective or unclear signage

8.3 Appeal Review

  • Appeals reviewed by Parking Appeals Committee
  • Decision rendered within 10 business days
  • Written notification of decision provided
  • Committee decisions are final

9. Special Circumstances

9.1 Temporary Permits

Temporary permits available for:

  • Visitors (daily permits from Campus Security)
  • Special events (event organizer coordinates with Transportation)
  • Temporary vehicle changes (bring documentation)
  • Medical/disability accommodations (with documentation)

9.2 Special Events

  • Parking restrictions may change during special events
  • Advance notice provided when possible
  • Event parking areas designated as needed
  • Follow directions of parking attendants

9.3 Weather Emergencies

  • Special parking rules may apply during weather emergencies
  • Follow instructions from Campus Security and University Communications
  • Do not block snow removal equipment access

10. Compliance and Review

This policy is reviewed annually by Transportation & Fleet Services. Parking fees and fine amounts are subject to annual review and adjustment. Questions or concerns should be directed to Transportation & Fleet Services.

11. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.7 (Physical Resources): The institution maintains physical facilities, including parking, adequate to support its mission.
  • Standard 13.8 (Institutional Environment): The institution provides a safe and secure campus environment.
