1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for reporting, managing, and administering workers' compensation claims at Wiley University. The purpose is to ensure that employees who suffer work-related injuries or illnesses receive timely and appropriate medical care and benefits while protecting the University from fraudulent claims and excessive costs.

(Reference: Texas Labor Code, Title 5 - Workers' Compensation; SACSCOC Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All full-time and part-time employees
  • Temporary and seasonal employees
  • Student employees (work-study and other student workers)
  • All supervisors and managers
  • Human Resources and Risk Management personnel

This policy does not apply to independent contractors, who are responsible for their own workers' compensation coverage.

3. Definitions

Work-Related Injury: An injury or illness arising out of and in the course of employment.

Occupational Disease: A disease or illness caused by conditions or activities of the workplace.

Workers' Compensation: A system of insurance providing wage replacement and medical benefits to employees injured during employment.

Lost Time: Work days missed due to a work-related injury or illness.

Return to Work: The process of returning an injured employee to productive employment.

Light Duty: Temporary modified job duties assigned to accommodate work restrictions.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University maintains workers' compensation insurance coverage as required by Texas law. The University is committed to providing a safe work environment and ensuring that employees who suffer work-related injuries receive prompt medical attention and appropriate benefits. All employees and supervisors must report work-related injuries immediately and cooperate with the claims management process.

Important: All work-related injuries, no matter how minor, must be reported within 24 hours. Failure to report injuries promptly may jeopardize benefits and result in disciplinary action.

5. Procedures

5.1 Immediate Response to Injury

  1. Seek Medical Attention: If the injury is an emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room
  2. Notify Supervisor: Report the injury to your immediate supervisor as soon as possible
  3. First Aid: For minor injuries, use first aid supplies available in the workplace
  4. Non-Emergency Medical Care: Use the designated workers' compensation medical provider unless emergency care is required

5.2 Reporting Requirements

Who Action Required Timeframe
Injured Employee Report injury to supervisor Immediately, no later than 24 hours
Supervisor Complete Supervisor's Incident Report Within 24 hours of notification
Supervisor Submit report to Risk Management Same day as incident report
Risk Management File claim with insurance carrier Within 8 days of injury
Employee Complete Employee Claim Form (DWC-41) Within 30 days of injury

5.3 Medical Treatment

  • Use the University's designated workers' compensation medical provider when possible
  • Emergency care may be obtained at any facility
  • Follow-up care should be with the designated provider unless otherwise authorized
  • Provide copies of all medical documentation to Risk Management
  • Attend all scheduled medical appointments
  • Follow physician's treatment plan and work restrictions

5.4 Employee Responsibilities

  • Report all injuries immediately, regardless of severity
  • Seek appropriate medical treatment
  • Complete all required paperwork accurately and promptly
  • Cooperate with the claims investigation
  • Provide medical updates and work status reports to supervisor
  • Return to work as soon as medically released
  • Perform light duty work if offered and within medical restrictions

5.5 Supervisor Responsibilities

  • Ensure injured employee receives immediate medical attention
  • Complete Supervisor's Incident Report within 24 hours
  • Investigate the incident and identify causes
  • Implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence
  • Maintain contact with injured employee during absence
  • Coordinate light duty assignments with HR and Risk Management
  • Document all communications regarding the injury

6. Workers' Compensation Benefits

6.1 Medical Benefits

  • Reasonable and necessary medical treatment related to the work injury
  • Prescription medications
  • Physical therapy and rehabilitation
  • Medical devices and equipment
  • Travel expenses to and from medical appointments

6.2 Income Benefits

Benefit Type Description
Temporary Income Benefits (TIBs) Partial wage replacement during recovery; begins after 7 days of disability
Impairment Income Benefits (IIBs) Benefits for permanent impairment based on impairment rating
Supplemental Income Benefits (SIBs) Additional benefits for employees unable to return to pre-injury earnings
Lifetime Income Benefits (LIBs) Benefits for specific severe injuries
Death Benefits Benefits for eligible dependents if employee dies from work-related injury

6.3 Waiting Period

There is a 7-day waiting period before income benefits begin. If the disability extends beyond 14 days, the first 7 days are paid retroactively.

7. Return to Work Program

Wiley University maintains an active Return to Work program to help injured employees return to productive employment as soon as medically appropriate.

7.1 Light Duty Assignments

  • Temporary modified duties may be offered within medical restrictions
  • Light duty may be in the employee's department or another department
  • Light duty assignments are temporary (typically up to 90 days)
  • Employees are expected to accept light duty if offered
  • Refusal of suitable light duty may affect benefits

7.2 Return to Work Process

  1. Physician provides written release with any work restrictions
  2. Employee provides release to supervisor and Risk Management
  3. Supervisor, HR, and Risk Management review restrictions and identify suitable work
  4. Employee returns to work under modified or regular duties
  5. Progress monitored until full duty release

8. Fraud Prevention

Workers' compensation fraud is a serious offense. The following are prohibited:

  • Filing false or exaggerated claims
  • Working while receiving disability benefits without authorization
  • Misrepresenting the nature or cause of an injury
  • Providing false information about work history or prior injuries

Suspected fraud will be investigated and may result in termination, denial of benefits, and criminal prosecution.

9. Confidentiality

Workers' compensation records are confidential and maintained separately from personnel files. Information is shared only with:

  • Those with a legitimate need to know
  • Insurance carriers and claims administrators
  • Medical providers treating the injury
  • Legal counsel as appropriate
  • Government agencies as required by law

10. Non-Retaliation

Wiley University prohibits retaliation against employees who report work-related injuries or file workers' compensation claims in good faith. Employees who believe they have experienced retaliation should report concerns to Human Resources.

11. Compliance and Review

This policy is reviewed annually by Risk Management and Human Resources to ensure compliance with Texas workers' compensation laws and best practices. Claims data is analyzed to identify trends and improve workplace safety.

12. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and properly manages its liabilities, including workers' compensation obligations.
  • Standard 13.8 (Institutional Environment): The institution provides a safe and secure environment for employees.
