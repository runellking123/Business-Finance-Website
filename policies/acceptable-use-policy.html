1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the appropriate use of Wiley University's information technology resources, including computers, networks, software, and data. The purpose is to protect the security, integrity, and availability of university technology resources while supporting the educational, research, and administrative mission of the institution.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.7 - Physical Resources; EDUCAUSE Security Guidelines)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All students, faculty, staff, and administrators
  • Contractors, vendors, and guests using university technology resources
  • All university-owned computers, devices, and equipment
  • University networks, including wired and wireless connections
  • University email, software, and cloud services
  • Personal devices connected to university networks
  • All data created, stored, or transmitted using university resources

3. Definitions

Information Technology Resources: All computing, networking, and electronic communication systems owned or operated by the University.

User: Any individual authorized to access university IT resources.

Sensitive Data: Information requiring protection due to legal requirements or institutional policy (FERPA data, financial records, SSNs, etc.).

Authentication Credentials: Usernames, passwords, PINs, or other means of verifying identity.

Malware: Software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University provides information technology resources to support its educational mission. Users are expected to use these resources responsibly, ethically, and in compliance with applicable laws and university policies. Access to IT resources is a privilege that may be revoked for policy violations.

4.1 Acceptable Uses

University IT resources may be used for:

  • Educational activities, including coursework and research
  • Official university business and administrative functions
  • Professional development and scholarly communication
  • Limited personal use that does not interfere with university operations
  • Authorized external access for academic partnerships

4.2 Prohibited Uses

The following uses are strictly prohibited:

  • Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content
  • Harassment, cyberbullying, or threatening communications
  • Unauthorized access to systems, accounts, or data
  • Sharing authentication credentials with others
  • Installing unauthorized software on university devices
  • Attempting to bypass security controls or firewalls
  • Using university resources for commercial purposes without authorization
  • Violating copyright laws through unauthorized file sharing
  • Creating, distributing, or executing malware
  • Sending spam or mass unsolicited emails
  • Impersonating others or misrepresenting identity
  • Using resources for political campaigning
  • Accessing or distributing pornographic materials
  • Gambling or gaming for money
  • Mining cryptocurrency without authorization

5. User Responsibilities

5.1 Account Security

  • Create strong passwords meeting university requirements
  • Change passwords when required or if compromise is suspected
  • Never share passwords or authentication credentials
  • Use multi-factor authentication where available
  • Log off or lock workstations when leaving them unattended
  • Report suspected security incidents to IT immediately

5.2 Data Protection

  • Handle sensitive data according to data classification guidelines
  • Store sensitive data only in approved, secure locations
  • Do not transmit sensitive data via unencrypted email
  • Report data breaches or suspected breaches immediately
  • Comply with FERPA, HIPAA, and other data protection regulations

5.3 Software and Applications

  • Install only approved and licensed software on university devices
  • Keep software and operating systems updated with security patches
  • Use only legitimate, licensed copies of software
  • Report software licensing concerns to IT

5.4 Network Usage

  • Connect only authorized devices to university networks
  • Do not attempt to intercept network traffic
  • Do not operate unauthorized servers or network services
  • Respect bandwidth limitations and avoid excessive consumption
  • Follow wireless network security requirements

6. Email and Communication

6.1 Email Use

  • Use university email for official university communications
  • Do not open suspicious attachments or click unknown links
  • Do not use email for sending sensitive unencrypted data
  • Maintain professional standards in email communications
  • Email is not private; it may be accessed for legitimate business purposes

6.2 Social Media

  • Do not represent personal views as official university positions
  • Respect confidentiality of university information
  • Do not harass, defame, or threaten others
  • Follow department social media guidelines when posting officially

7. Privacy and Monitoring

Users should have no expectation of privacy when using university IT resources. The University reserves the right to:

  • Monitor network traffic for security purposes
  • Access user files and communications when required by law or policy
  • Audit system usage and access logs
  • Investigate potential policy violations
  • Preserve evidence for legal proceedings

Monitoring will be conducted in accordance with applicable laws and university policies.

8. Personal Devices (BYOD)

Personal devices connecting to university networks must:

  • Have current antivirus software installed
  • Have operating system and security updates applied
  • Use secure wireless connections (avoid public hotspots for sensitive work)
  • Not be used to store university sensitive data without encryption
  • Be reported to IT if lost or stolen when containing university data

9. Copyright and Intellectual Property

  • Respect copyright laws when using, copying, or distributing materials
  • Do not use peer-to-peer software for illegal file sharing
  • Properly cite and attribute sources in academic work
  • Follow software licensing agreements
  • Report suspected copyright violations

(Reference: Digital Millennium Copyright Act; Higher Education Opportunity Act)

10. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:

  • Temporary or permanent loss of IT access privileges
  • Disciplinary action under Student Code of Conduct or Employee Handbook
  • Academic sanctions including failing grades
  • Termination of employment
  • Civil or criminal prosecution

11. Incident Reporting

Users must report the following to the IT Help Desk immediately:

  • Suspected security breaches or unauthorized access
  • Lost or stolen devices containing university data
  • Suspected malware infections
  • Phishing attempts or suspicious communications
  • Violations of this policy by others

12. Compliance and Review

This policy is reviewed annually by Information Technology in consultation with legal counsel to ensure compliance with current laws, regulations, and best practices. All users must acknowledge this policy as a condition of receiving IT access.

13. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 12.4 (Student Records): The institution protects student rights to privacy of student records.
