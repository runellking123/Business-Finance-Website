1. Purpose

This policy establishes the guidelines and procedures for the procurement of goods and services at Wiley University. The purpose is to ensure that all purchases are made in a manner that is fiscally responsible, compliant with applicable laws and regulations, and in the best interest of the University while maintaining appropriate internal controls.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources; Standard 13.6 - Federal and State Responsibilities)

2. Scope

This policy applies to all purchases of goods and services made on behalf of Wiley University, regardless of funding source. This includes purchases funded by:

  • Operating budget funds
  • Auxiliary enterprise funds
  • Federal and state grants and contracts
  • Restricted and unrestricted gifts
  • Capital project funds

All employees involved in the procurement process must adhere to this policy.

3. Definitions

Purchase Order (PO): A formal document issued by the Business Office authorizing a vendor to provide goods or services at a specified price.

Purchase Requisition: An internal document requesting authorization to purchase goods or services.

Competitive Bidding: The process of soliciting bids from multiple vendors to obtain the best value.

Sole Source: A procurement where only one vendor can provide the required goods or services.

P-Card: A university-issued purchasing card for small-dollar transactions.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University is committed to procuring goods and services through fair, open, and competitive processes that ensure the best value while maintaining compliance with all applicable regulations. All purchases must be necessary, reasonable, and serve a legitimate business purpose.

4.1 Purchasing Thresholds and Requirements

Purchase Amount Requirements Approval Authority
$0 - $500 P-Card or Petty Cash; no PO required Department Head
$501 - $2,500 Purchase Order required; price comparison recommended Department Head
$2,501 - $10,000 Purchase Order required; minimum 3 written quotes Controller
$10,001 - $50,000 Formal competitive bidding; minimum 3 sealed bids SVP for Business & Finance
Over $50,000 Formal sealed bid or RFP process President / Board of Trustees

4.2 Competitive Bidding Exemptions

Competitive bidding may be waived under the following circumstances with documented justification:

  • Sole Source: Only one vendor can provide the item or service
  • Emergency Purchases: Immediate threat to health, safety, or university operations
  • Cooperative Purchasing: Use of state, regional, or national cooperative contracts
  • Professional Services: Services requiring specialized expertise (legal, audit, consulting)
  • Standardization: Compatibility with existing equipment or systems

All exemptions must be documented and approved by the SVP for Business & Finance.

4.3 Prohibited Purchases

University funds may not be used for:

  • Personal items or services for individual benefit
  • Alcoholic beverages (except with President's approval for official events)
  • Political contributions or campaign materials
  • Gifts to employees (except for approved recognition programs)
  • Items or services from suspended or debarred vendors

5. Procedures and Responsibilities

5.1 Requesting a Purchase

  1. Requestor identifies need and confirms budget availability
  2. Requestor completes Purchase Requisition form
  3. Department Head reviews and approves requisition
  4. Business Office reviews for compliance and issues Purchase Order
  5. Vendor fulfills order; requestor verifies receipt of goods/services
  6. Invoice processed for payment after three-way match

5.2 Vendor Selection

  • Vendors must be registered in the university's vendor database
  • New vendors must complete W-9 and vendor registration forms
  • Preference given to vendors offering educational discounts
  • Conflict of interest disclosures required for vendor relationships
  • Vendors must not appear on federal debarment lists

5.3 Purchase Order Processing

  • Purchase Orders must be issued before goods are ordered or services rendered
  • Retroactive POs are discouraged and require Controller approval
  • POs must include complete specifications, pricing, and delivery terms
  • Changes to POs require formal amendments

5.4 Receiving and Payment

  • Departments must inspect and document receipt of goods/services
  • Discrepancies must be reported to vendor and Business Office immediately
  • Invoices must be submitted to Business Office within 5 business days of receipt
  • Payment processed after verification (three-way match: PO, receiving report, invoice)

6. Special Purchasing Situations

6.1 Federal Grant Purchases

Purchases using federal funds must comply with 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance), including:

  • Additional documentation requirements
  • Buy American provisions where applicable
  • Prohibition on certain telecommunications equipment
  • Cost principles for allowability

(Reference: 2 CFR Part 200 - Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements)

6.2 Capital Equipment

Equipment purchases over $5,000 with a useful life exceeding one year must be:

  • Tagged and added to the capital asset inventory
  • Approved through the capital budget process for items over $25,000
  • Subject to disposition procedures when no longer needed

6.3 Technology Purchases

All technology purchases (hardware, software, cloud services) must be reviewed and approved by Information Technology for:

  • Compatibility with university systems
  • Security compliance
  • Support and maintenance requirements
  • Data privacy considerations

7. Compliance and Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:

  • Return of goods at the employee's expense
  • Personal liability for unauthorized purchases
  • Revocation of purchasing authority
  • Disciplinary action up to and including termination

8. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and a demonstrated, stable financial base to support its mission.
  • Standard 13.6 (Federal and State Responsibilities): The institution complies with applicable federal and state requirements for the proper management and administration of financial resources.
