1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the establishment, use, and management of petty cash funds at Wiley University. Petty cash funds provide a convenient method for making small, incidental purchases when other payment methods are not practical or efficient.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources)

2. Scope

This policy applies to all departments and personnel authorized to maintain or access petty cash funds. It governs the establishment, disbursement, replenishment, and audit of all petty cash funds at the University.

3. Definitions

Petty Cash Fund: A fixed amount of cash maintained by a department for making small purchases and reimbursements.

Custodian: The individual designated as responsible for the safekeeping and proper use of a petty cash fund.

Imprest System: A system where the fund balance plus receipts and vouchers always equals the established fund amount.

4. Policy Statement

4.1 Fund Establishment

  • Petty cash funds may be established with approval from the Controller
  • Standard fund amounts are $100, $200, or $300 based on departmental need
  • Requests for larger amounts require SVP for Business & Finance approval
  • Each fund must have a designated custodian who signs a custodian agreement
  • Only one petty cash fund per department is permitted

4.2 Authorized Uses

Petty cash may be used for:

  • Small office supplies under $50
  • Postage and shipping costs
  • Minor emergency repairs
  • Local transportation (parking, tolls)
  • Refreshments for official meetings (with prior approval)
  • Other incidental business expenses under $100

4.3 Prohibited Uses

Petty cash may NOT be used for:

  • Personal expenses or loans to employees
  • Purchases exceeding $100 per transaction
  • Cashing personal checks
  • Regular, recurring purchases (use P-Card or Purchase Order)
  • Travel advances
  • Gift cards or cash equivalents
  • Alcoholic beverages
  • Items available through central purchasing at lower cost

5. Procedures and Responsibilities

5.1 Custodian Responsibilities

  • Maintain fund in a secure, locked location
  • Disburse funds only for authorized purposes with proper documentation
  • Obtain receipts for all disbursements
  • Prepare Petty Cash Vouchers for each transaction
  • Request replenishment when fund reaches 25% of original amount
  • Reconcile fund monthly and upon request
  • Report any discrepancies immediately to the Business Office
  • Never commingle personal funds with petty cash

5.2 Disbursement Process

  1. Employee requests petty cash reimbursement or advance
  2. Custodian verifies request is for authorized purpose
  3. Employee provides receipt or signs advance acknowledgment
  4. Custodian completes Petty Cash Voucher
  5. Custodian disburses cash and retains voucher with receipt
  6. For advances, employee returns receipt and change within 3 business days

5.3 Replenishment Process

  1. Custodian completes Petty Cash Replenishment Request
  2. Attach all vouchers and receipts in order
  3. Verify cash on hand plus vouchers equals fund amount
  4. Submit to Business Office with account codes for each expense
  5. Business Office reviews, approves, and issues replenishment check
  6. Custodian cashes check and restores fund to original amount

5.4 Documentation Requirements

All petty cash disbursements require:

  • Original receipt (itemized when possible)
  • Completed Petty Cash Voucher
  • Business purpose explanation
  • Signature of person receiving funds
  • Approval signature for amounts over $50

6. Security and Audit

6.1 Physical Security

  • Petty cash must be kept in a locked box or drawer
  • Only the custodian should have access to the fund
  • Fund should never be left unattended or unsecured
  • Do not store petty cash with other cash or valuables

6.2 Audits and Counts

  • Custodian must reconcile fund monthly
  • Business Office may conduct unannounced audits
  • Annual verification required for all funds
  • Shortages must be reported and investigated immediately
  • Repeated discrepancies may result in fund closure

6.3 Custodian Changes

When a custodian changes:

  • Business Office must be notified in advance
  • Outgoing and incoming custodians count fund together
  • Both sign transfer documentation
  • New custodian signs custodian agreement

7. Fund Closure

To close a petty cash fund:

  • Submit all outstanding vouchers for replenishment
  • Return remaining cash to Business Office
  • Complete fund closure form
  • Business Office verifies total and closes fund account

8. Compliance

Violations of this policy may result in:

  • Personal liability for shortages
  • Revocation of petty cash privileges
  • Disciplinary action

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 requires sound financial practices and controls)
