1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework and procedures for the development, approval, and management of the annual operating budget at Wiley University. A sound budgeting process ensures the institution allocates resources effectively to support its mission, strategic goals, and educational programs while maintaining fiscal responsibility.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources; Standard 7.1 - Institutional Planning)

2. Scope

This policy applies to all university departments, divisions, and auxiliary operations involved in the budget planning and management process. It encompasses:

  • Operating budgets (unrestricted funds)
  • Auxiliary enterprise budgets
  • Restricted fund budgets
  • Capital budgets
  • Grant and contract budgets

3. Definitions

Operating Budget: The annual financial plan for revenues and expenditures related to the University's ongoing operations.

Capital Budget: The plan for acquisition, construction, or renovation of major assets and infrastructure.

Budget Unit: A department, program, or cost center with an assigned budget.

Budget Manager: The individual responsible for managing a budget unit's resources.

Variance: The difference between budgeted and actual amounts.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University maintains a comprehensive budget process that aligns resource allocation with institutional priorities, ensures transparency and accountability, and supports the University's mission and strategic plan. The budget process shall:

  • Be inclusive and participatory, involving appropriate stakeholders
  • Align with the University's strategic plan and accreditation requirements
  • Ensure revenues are sufficient to support planned expenditures
  • Maintain appropriate reserves for contingencies
  • Comply with all regulatory and fiduciary requirements

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 7.1 requires integrated planning and resource allocation)

5.2 Revenue Projections

Revenue projections shall be developed using:

  • Enrollment projections from Admissions and Registrar
  • Approved tuition and fee rates
  • Historical revenue trends and collection rates
  • Anticipated grant and contract awards
  • Auxiliary service revenue estimates
  • Investment income projections
  • Gift and endowment projections from Advancement

5.3 Expenditure Planning

Expenditure budgets shall include:

  • Personnel Costs: Salaries, wages, benefits, and approved position changes
  • Operating Expenses: Supplies, services, travel, and contractual obligations
  • Capital Expenditures: Equipment, technology, and facility improvements
  • Debt Service: Principal and interest on outstanding obligations
  • Transfers: Mandatory and discretionary transfers to/from reserves

6. Budget Management

6.1 Budget Authority

Action Approval Authority
Annual budget adoption Board of Trustees
Budget transfers within division (under $10,000) Budget Manager
Budget transfers within division ($10,000-$50,000) Division Head
Budget transfers between divisions SVP for Business & Finance
New position authorization President
Budget amendments over $100,000 Board of Trustees

6.2 Budget Monitoring

  • Budget managers receive monthly budget-to-actual reports
  • Variances exceeding 10% require written explanation
  • Quarterly budget reviews conducted with each division
  • Mid-year budget revisions prepared if significant changes occur
  • Year-end projections submitted in April

6.3 Deficit Prevention

  • Departments may not exceed their approved budgets without authorization
  • Expenditures are encumbered against budgets when committed
  • Projected deficits must be reported immediately with corrective action plan
  • Positions may be frozen to prevent budget overruns

7. Reserve Requirements

The University shall maintain reserves sufficient to:

  • Meet operating cash flow needs
  • Address unforeseen emergencies
  • Support strategic initiatives
  • Satisfy debt covenant requirements

Target reserve levels shall be established by the Board of Trustees and reviewed annually.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 requires demonstrated financial stability)

8. Roles and Responsibilities

8.1 Board of Trustees

  • Approves annual operating and capital budgets
  • Establishes financial policies and reserve requirements
  • Reviews quarterly financial reports
  • Approves significant budget amendments

8.2 President

  • Recommends budget to Board of Trustees
  • Establishes institutional budget priorities
  • Authorizes new positions and major expenditures

8.3 Senior Vice President for Business & Finance

  • Directs the budget development process
  • Issues budget guidelines and instructions
  • Reviews and consolidates departmental requests
  • Monitors budget performance institution-wide

8.4 Budget Managers

  • Develop and submit departmental budget requests
  • Manage resources within approved budgets
  • Monitor and report budget variances
  • Implement corrective actions as needed

9. Compliance and Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually and updated to reflect changes in institutional needs, regulatory requirements, or best practices. The budget process shall be evaluated periodically to ensure it supports effective resource allocation and institutional planning.

10. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 7.1 (Institutional Planning): The institution engages in ongoing, comprehensive, and integrated research-based planning and evaluation processes.
  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and a demonstrated, stable financial base to support its mission.
  • Standard 13.2 (Financial Documents): The institution has a sound financial base and financial stability demonstrated by audited financial statements.
