1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the issuance, use, and control of keys and access devices at Wiley University. Proper key control is essential for protecting university property, ensuring the safety of students, faculty, and staff, and maintaining secure access to university facilities.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.7 - Physical Resources)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All employees (full-time, part-time, and temporary)
  • Contractors and vendors requiring building access
  • Student workers and graduate assistants
  • Student organization leaders with authorized access
  • All university-owned and leased facilities

3. Definitions

Master Key: A key that opens multiple locks within a building or system.

Sub-Master Key: A key that opens a specific group of locks within a larger system.

Individual Key: A key that opens only one specific lock.

Access Card: An electronic card or fob used to access electronically controlled doors.

Key Holder: Any person issued a university key or access device.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University maintains strict control over all keys and access devices to protect campus assets and ensure the safety of the campus community. Keys are university property and must be returned upon request or when employment or affiliation ends.

4.1 Key Classification

Key Type Description Approval Required
Grand Master Opens all locks on campus President only
Building Master Opens all locks in a specific building SVP for Business & Finance
Department Sub-Master Opens all locks within a department Division Head
Individual/Office Key Opens one specific room Department Head
Electronic Access Card Activates electronic door locks Department Head

5. Procedures

5.1 Key Request Process

  1. Supervisor completes Key Request Form with justification
  2. Appropriate approval authority signs request
  3. Request submitted to Facilities Management
  4. Facilities verifies approval and availability
  5. Key holder signs Key Receipt acknowledging responsibilities
  6. Key issued and recorded in key control database

5.2 Key Holder Responsibilities

  • Keep keys in personal possession at all times
  • Never lend keys to another person
  • Never duplicate university keys
  • Never leave keys unattended or in an unlocked vehicle
  • Report lost or stolen keys immediately to Facilities Management
  • Return all keys upon termination, transfer, or when no longer needed
  • Lock doors when leaving secured areas

5.3 Lost or Stolen Keys

  • Report loss immediately to Facilities Management and Campus Security
  • Complete Lost Key Report Form
  • A replacement fee may be charged for lost keys
  • Lock re-keying may be required at the department's expense
  • Repeated losses may result in denial of future key requests

5.4 Key Return Process

  • Return all keys to Facilities Management, not supervisors
  • Obtain signed receipt for returned keys
  • Final paycheck may be held until keys are returned
  • Unreturned keys may result in lock replacement charges

6. Electronic Access Control

6.1 Access Card Issuance

  • Access cards issued by Campus Security or IT
  • Photo ID required for card issuance
  • Access levels determined by job responsibilities
  • Cards are non-transferable

6.2 Access Levels

Level Access Type Typical Use
1 General Access Main entrances during business hours
2 Building Access Assigned building after hours
3 Department Access Department areas including after hours
4 Restricted Access Secure areas (server rooms, labs, storage)
5 All Access Campus-wide 24/7 access

6.3 Access Card Deactivation

  • Cards deactivated immediately upon termination notification
  • Lost cards deactivated when reported
  • Inactive cards deactivated after 90 days of no use
  • HR notifies Security of all terminations within 24 hours

7. Temporary and Contractor Access

7.1 Temporary Employees

  • Issued minimum access necessary for job duties
  • Keys issued for assignment duration only
  • Sponsoring department responsible for key return

7.2 Contractors and Vendors

  • Must be escorted in secure areas unless pre-approved
  • Temporary access cards issued for project duration
  • Facilities Management maintains contractor access log
  • Contractor must sign access agreement

8. Audit and Compliance

  • Annual audit of all issued keys conducted by Facilities Management
  • Key holders must present keys for verification upon request
  • Access card usage logs reviewed quarterly
  • Discrepancies reported to department heads for resolution

9. Violations and Consequences

Violations of this policy may result in:

  • Revocation of key privileges
  • Financial liability for re-keying costs
  • Disciplinary action up to and including termination
  • Criminal charges for theft or unauthorized entry

10. Roles and Responsibilities

10.1 Facilities Management

  • Maintains key control database and records
  • Issues and collects all physical keys
  • Conducts annual key audits
  • Coordinates lock changes and re-keying

10.2 Campus Security

  • Issues and manages electronic access cards
  • Monitors access control systems
  • Investigates reports of lost or stolen keys
  • Deactivates access for terminated employees

10.3 Department Heads

  • Approve key requests for departmental staff
  • Ensure timely key return when employees transfer or leave
  • Report access needs for contractors working in their areas

11. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.7 (Physical Resources): The institution maintains physical facilities that are adequate to support its mission and operations.
  • Standard 13.8 (Institutional Environment): The institution provides a safe and secure environment for students, faculty, and staff.
