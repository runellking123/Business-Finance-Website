1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the acquisition, tagging, tracking, and disposal of university property and equipment. Proper inventory management ensures accountability for institutional assets, compliance with financial reporting requirements, and protection of university resources.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources; 2 CFR 200.313 - Equipment)

2. Scope

This policy applies to all capital and controlled property acquired by the University, including:

  • Equipment purchased with university operating funds
  • Equipment purchased with federal grants and contracts
  • Equipment purchased with restricted gifts
  • Donated equipment accepted by the University
  • Leased equipment with purchase options
  • Property at all university locations, including off-site facilities

3. Definitions

Capital Equipment: Tangible property with a useful life exceeding one year and an acquisition cost of $5,000 or more.

Controlled Property: Items with acquisition cost between $500 and $4,999 that are sensitive, portable, or theft-prone (computers, cameras, projectors).

Inventory Tag: A numbered label affixed to equipment for identification and tracking purposes.

Custodian: The department or individual responsible for the care and safekeeping of assigned property.

Useful Life: The estimated period an asset is expected to be functional for its intended purpose.

Depreciation: The systematic allocation of an asset's cost over its useful life.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University maintains accurate and complete records of all capital and controlled property to ensure proper stewardship of institutional resources, accurate financial reporting, and compliance with sponsor requirements for federally funded equipment.

4.1 Capitalization Thresholds

Property Type Threshold Tracking Required
Capital Equipment $5,000 or more Full inventory tracking, depreciation
Controlled Property $500 - $4,999 Inventory tracking, no depreciation
Supplies/Expendables Under $500 Departmental records only
Federally Funded Equipment $5,000 or more Per federal requirements (2 CFR 200.313)

5. Acquisition and Tagging

5.1 New Equipment Acquisition

  • Purchasing follows the University Purchasing Policy
  • Business Office reviews all purchases over $5,000 for capitalization
  • Equipment received is reported to Business Office within 5 business days
  • Inventory tag affixed before equipment is put into service

5.2 Donated Equipment

  • Donations accepted per the Gift Acceptance Policy
  • Fair market value documented at time of receipt
  • Items meeting thresholds tagged and entered into inventory
  • Advancement Office coordinates with Business Office on valuations

5.3 Inventory Tag Requirements

  • Tags placed in visible, accessible location on equipment
  • Tags not to be removed or defaced
  • Missing or damaged tags reported to Business Office
  • Replacement tags issued as needed

6. Inventory Record Information

The following information is maintained for each inventoried item:

  • University inventory tag number
  • Description and manufacturer
  • Model and serial number
  • Acquisition date and cost
  • Funding source (especially for grant-funded items)
  • Location (building and room)
  • Custodial department
  • Condition and status
  • Useful life and depreciation information

7. Custodian Responsibilities

  • Safeguard equipment from theft, damage, and misuse
  • Ensure equipment is used only for authorized purposes
  • Notify Business Office of equipment location changes
  • Participate in annual physical inventory verification
  • Report lost, stolen, or damaged equipment immediately
  • Ensure equipment is properly maintained
  • Do not dispose of or surplus equipment without authorization

8. Equipment Transfers

8.1 Internal Transfers

  • Complete Equipment Transfer Form before moving equipment
  • Both releasing and receiving departments sign form
  • Submit to Business Office for inventory update
  • Federally funded equipment may require sponsor approval

8.2 Off-Campus Equipment

  • Equipment removed from campus requires written authorization
  • Complete Off-Campus Equipment Agreement
  • Equipment remains university property
  • User responsible for loss or damage
  • Return required upon separation from university

9. Physical Inventory

9.1 Annual Inventory

  • Business Office conducts annual physical inventory
  • All capital equipment must be physically verified
  • Departments receive inventory listing for verification
  • Discrepancies investigated and resolved
  • Inventory certification signed by department head

9.2 Federal Equipment Inventory

Equipment purchased with federal funds is inventoried per 2 CFR 200.313:

  • Physical inventory conducted at least every two years
  • Control system ensures adequate safeguards
  • Maintenance procedures keep equipment in good condition
  • Records maintained for three years after disposition

(Reference: 2 CFR 200.313 - Equipment)

10. Lost, Stolen, or Damaged Property

  • Report immediately to Campus Security and Business Office
  • Complete Property Loss Report Form
  • Police report required for theft over $500
  • Investigation conducted to determine circumstances
  • Insurance claim filed if applicable
  • Accountability assigned if negligence determined

11. Equipment Disposal

11.1 Surplus Property

  • Equipment no longer needed identified as surplus
  • Complete Surplus Property Request Form
  • Business Office determines appropriate disposal method
  • Options include: transfer to another department, sale, donation, or disposal

11.2 Disposal Methods

Method Requirements
Internal Transfer First priority for usable equipment
Public Sale/Auction Items with residual value; proceeds to university
Donation To qualified non-profit organizations
Trade-In Credit toward new equipment purchase
Recycling Electronic waste disposed per environmental regulations
Disposal Items with no value; documented destruction

11.3 Federal Equipment Disposal

Equipment purchased with federal funds requires:

  • Sponsor notification if original cost exceeded $5,000
  • Sponsor approval before sale or transfer outside university
  • Proceeds may be owed to the federal government

12. Depreciation

Capital equipment is depreciated using the straight-line method over the asset's useful life:

Asset Category Useful Life
Computer Equipment 5 years
Office Equipment 7 years
Laboratory Equipment 10 years
Vehicles 5 years
Furniture 10 years
Buildings 40 years

13. Compliance and Review

This policy is reviewed annually by the Business Office to ensure compliance with federal regulations, GAAP requirements, and accreditation standards. The inventory system is subject to review during annual financial audits.

14. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and properly accounts for its assets.
  • Standard 13.2 (Financial Documents): The institution prepares accurate financial statements in accordance with GAAP.
  • Standard 13.7 (Physical Resources): The institution maintains physical facilities adequate to support its mission.
