1. Purpose

This policy establishes the guidelines and procedures for refunds of tuition, fees, and other charges at Wiley University. The purpose is to ensure equitable treatment of students who withdraw or reduce their course load while maintaining institutional financial stability and compliance with federal, state, and accreditation requirements.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 12.3 - Tuition, Fees, and Other Student Charges; 34 CFR 668.22 - Return of Title IV Funds)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All enrolled students who withdraw from courses or the University
  • Students who reduce their course load after the semester begins
  • Students receiving financial aid subject to Return of Title IV Funds requirements
  • Students dismissed for academic or disciplinary reasons
  • Students called to active military duty

3. Definitions

Withdrawal: The official process of dropping all courses and ending enrollment for the term.

Drop: Removing one or more courses while remaining enrolled in at least one course.

Refund: A return of tuition, fees, or other charges to the student or applicable financial aid programs.

Credit Balance: Funds remaining on a student account after all charges have been satisfied.

Title IV Funds: Federal financial aid including Pell Grants, SEOG, Direct Loans, and PLUS Loans.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University provides tuition and fee refunds based on the date of withdrawal or course drop as documented by the Registrar's Office. The refund schedule is designed to be fair and consistent while recognizing the University's commitment of resources at the start of each term.

5. Procedures

5.1 Official Withdrawal Process

  1. Student completes Withdrawal Form with the Registrar's Office
  2. Registrar records withdrawal date and notifies Student Accounts
  3. Student Accounts calculates refund based on withdrawal date
  4. Financial Aid Office calculates Return of Title IV Funds (if applicable)
  5. Refund processed within 14 business days of withdrawal date

5.2 Dropping Individual Courses

  • Students dropping courses while remaining enrolled receive prorated tuition adjustments
  • Adjustments follow the same refund schedule as full withdrawals
  • Course fees are refundable only if dropped before the first day of class
  • Dropping below full-time status may affect financial aid eligibility

5.3 Unofficial Withdrawal

Students who stop attending without officially withdrawing are considered unofficially withdrawn. The withdrawal date is determined by:

  • Last date of academic activity as documented by faculty
  • If no date can be determined, the midpoint of the semester is used

6. Refund Distribution

6.1 Students with Financial Aid

When a student receiving financial aid withdraws, refunds are applied in the following order as required by federal regulations:

  1. Unsubsidized Federal Direct Loans
  2. Subsidized Federal Direct Loans
  3. Federal Direct PLUS Loans
  4. Federal Pell Grants
  5. Federal SEOG Grants
  6. Other federal Title IV aid
  7. State grants and scholarships
  8. Institutional scholarships and grants
  9. Student

(Reference: 34 CFR 668.22(i) - Order of return of Title IV funds)

6.2 Students without Financial Aid

Students who paid out-of-pocket receive refunds directly by:

  • Direct deposit (if enrolled)
  • Paper check mailed to address on file

7. Credit Balance Refunds

When financial aid or other payments exceed charges, a credit balance results:

  • Title IV credit balances are refunded within 14 days of the credit creation
  • Students may authorize credit balances to be held for future charges
  • Parents who received PLUS Loans receive refunds unless student is authorized
  • Credit balances at the end of the academic year are refunded to the student

(Reference: 34 CFR 668.164(h) - Credit balance requirements)

8. Non-Refundable Charges

The following charges are generally non-refundable:

  • Application fees
  • Late registration fees
  • Returned check fees
  • Parking fines and other penalties
  • Charges for lost or damaged property
  • Books and supplies that have been used

9. Special Circumstances

9.1 Military Withdrawal

Students called to active military duty are eligible for:

  • Full refund of tuition and mandatory fees for the term
  • Prorated refund of room and board charges
  • Option to receive incomplete grades and return to complete coursework

Documentation of military orders is required.

9.2 Medical Withdrawal

Students withdrawing for documented medical reasons may request consideration for additional refund through the Appeals Committee. Required documentation includes:

  • Completed Medical Withdrawal Form
  • Documentation from a licensed healthcare provider
  • Recommendation from the Dean of Students

9.3 University-Initiated Cancellations

When the University cancels a course or program, students receive:

  • Full refund of tuition for the cancelled course
  • Full refund of any course-specific fees
  • Prorated refund of mandatory fees if all courses are cancelled

9.4 Deceased Students

In the event of a student's death, the University will:

  • Cancel all outstanding charges for the current term
  • Refund payments made for the current term to the estate
  • Work with financial aid to minimize impact on the family

10. Appeals Process

Students who believe their circumstances warrant an exception to this policy may submit a written appeal to the Student Accounts Appeals Committee. Appeals must include:

  • Completed Refund Appeal Form
  • Explanation of extenuating circumstances
  • Supporting documentation

Appeals are reviewed within 15 business days. The Committee's decision is final.

11. Compliance and Review

This policy is reviewed annually to ensure compliance with federal regulations (34 CFR 668.22), state requirements, and SACSCOC standards. The Student Accounts Office maintains records of all refund transactions and Return of Title IV calculations.

12. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 12.3 (Tuition, Fees, and Other Student Charges): The institution publishes a clear and fair refund policy.
  • Standard 12.4 (Student Records): The institution maintains accurate financial records for all students.
  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and practices.
  • Standard 13.6 (Federal and State Responsibilities): The institution complies with applicable federal requirements for financial aid refunds.
