1. Purpose

This policy establishes the guidelines for the assessment, billing, collection, and adjustment of tuition and fees at Wiley University. The purpose is to ensure transparent, consistent, and equitable practices in student billing while maintaining compliance with federal, state, and accreditation requirements.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 12.3 - Tuition, Fees, and Other Student Charges; Standard 13.1 - Financial Resources)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All enrolled undergraduate and graduate students
  • Students in certificate and non-degree programs
  • Dual enrollment and concurrent enrollment students
  • Auditing students
  • Students enrolled in online and distance education programs

3. Definitions

Tuition: Charges assessed for enrollment in academic courses, typically calculated per credit hour or as a flat rate for full-time enrollment.

Mandatory Fees: Required fees assessed to all students for services and activities, regardless of individual usage.

Course Fees: Additional fees for specific courses requiring specialized materials, equipment, or instruction.

Census Date: The official date for enrollment reporting, typically the 12th class day of a semester.

Payment Plan: A structured arrangement allowing students to pay tuition and fees in installments.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University establishes tuition and fees that support the delivery of quality educational programs while remaining accessible to students. All charges are approved by the Board of Trustees, published in official university documents, and applied consistently to all students in accordance with their enrollment status.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 12.3 requires that charges are published and accurately represented)

4.1 Tuition Rate Approval

  • Tuition and mandatory fee rates are approved annually by the Board of Trustees
  • Rates are established during the spring semester for the following academic year
  • Rate changes are communicated to students at least 60 days before the effective date
  • Current rates are published in the University Catalog and on the Student Accounts website

4.2 Tuition Structure

Student Type Tuition Basis
Full-time Undergraduate (12-18 credit hours) Flat rate per semester
Part-time Undergraduate (below 12 credit hours) Per credit hour
Overload Undergraduate (above 18 credit hours) Additional per credit hour charge
Graduate Students Per credit hour
Online Programs Per credit hour (includes technology fee)

6. Special Fees and Charges

6.1 Course-Specific Fees

Additional fees may be assessed for courses requiring:

  • Laboratory materials and equipment
  • Studio or performance supplies
  • Clinical placement coordination
  • Field trip or travel expenses
  • Specialized software or technology

6.2 One-Time Fees

  • Application Fee: Non-refundable fee for processing admission applications
  • Enrollment Deposit: Confirms enrollment; applied to first semester charges
  • Graduation Fee: Covers diploma, regalia rental, and commencement costs
  • Transcript Fee: Charge for official transcript requests

6.3 Penalty Fees

  • Late Registration Fee: Assessed for registering after the deadline
  • Late Payment Fee: Assessed when payment is not received by due date
  • Returned Check Fee: Assessed for checks returned by the bank
  • Reinstatement Fee: Assessed when student is reinstated after being dropped for non-payment

7. Billing and Payment

7.1 Billing Schedule

  • Tuition bills are generated after registration and updated as changes occur
  • Electronic billing statements sent to university email accounts
  • Students may view real-time account status through the student portal
  • Payment due dates published in the academic calendar

7.2 Payment Methods

The University accepts:

  • Cash (in-person only)
  • Personal checks and money orders
  • Credit/debit cards (service fee may apply)
  • Electronic funds transfer (ACH)
  • Financial aid (grants, scholarships, loans)
  • Third-party billing (employer, agency)
  • 529 education savings plan distributions

7.3 Payment Plans

Interest-free payment plans are available for students who cannot pay the full balance by the due date:

  • Enrollment fee required to participate
  • Balance divided into equal monthly installments
  • Automatic payment enrollment required
  • Missed payments result in late fees and potential removal from plan

8. Financial Aid Application

  • Accepted financial aid is applied to student accounts when funds are received
  • Financial aid in excess of charges is refunded per the Refund Policy
  • Students are responsible for any balance not covered by financial aid
  • Estimated aid shown on billing statements; actual amounts may vary

(Reference: 34 CFR 668.164 - Disbursement of Title IV funds)

9. Delinquent Accounts

Students with past-due balances may be subject to:

  • Registration holds preventing enrollment in future terms
  • Transcript and diploma holds
  • Removal from classes if payment arrangements are not made
  • Referral to a collection agency
  • Reporting to credit bureaus
  • Additional collection costs and fees

10. Tuition Adjustments

Adjustments to tuition and fees follow the schedules outlined in the University's Refund Policy. Adjustments may be granted for:

  • Course withdrawals before published deadlines
  • Complete withdrawal from the University
  • Approved leaves of absence
  • University cancellation of courses
  • Documented billing errors

11. Special Circumstances

11.1 Military Service Members

Active duty military and their dependents may be eligible for:

  • In-state tuition rates regardless of residence
  • Military tuition assistance acceptance
  • Flexible payment arrangements during deployment
  • Tuition adjustments for military withdrawals

11.2 Texas Residency

Texas residency for tuition purposes is determined in accordance with Texas Education Code requirements. Students may petition for reclassification through the Registrar's Office.

12. Compliance and Review

This policy is reviewed annually to ensure compliance with federal and state regulations, accreditation requirements, and institutional needs. The Student Accounts Office maintains procedures to implement this policy consistently.

13. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 12.3 (Tuition, Fees, and Other Student Charges): The institution publishes accurate, current, and complete information regarding tuition, fees, and other charges in its official publication.
  • Standard 12.4 (Student Records): The institution protects student rights to privacy and provides access to their educational records.
  • Standard 13.1 (Financial Resources): The institution has sound financial resources and a demonstrated, stable financial base to support its mission.
