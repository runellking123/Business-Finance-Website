1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the safe and appropriate use of university-owned, leased, and rented vehicles, as well as personal vehicles used for university business. The purpose is to protect the safety of drivers, passengers, and the public while minimizing liability and ensuring proper stewardship of university resources.

(Reference: SACSCOC Standard 13.7 - Physical Resources; Texas Transportation Code)

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All employees operating vehicles for university business
  • Students authorized to drive university vehicles
  • Volunteers operating vehicles for university-sponsored activities
  • All university-owned and leased vehicles
  • Rental vehicles obtained for university business
  • Personal vehicles used for university business (mileage reimbursement)

3. Definitions

University Vehicle: Any motor vehicle owned, leased, or rented by Wiley University.

Authorized Driver: An individual who has been approved to operate university vehicles after meeting all requirements.

University Business: Travel or transportation directly related to university operations, programs, or activities.

Personal Vehicle: A motor vehicle owned or leased by an employee or student, not the University.

Motor Vehicle Record (MVR): An official driving history report from the state licensing authority.

4. Policy Statement

Wiley University is committed to providing safe transportation for university-related activities. Only authorized drivers may operate university vehicles, and all drivers must comply with traffic laws and safe driving practices. The University maintains appropriate insurance coverage for university vehicles and authorized drivers.

4.1 Driver Authorization Requirements

To be authorized to drive university vehicles, individuals must:

  • Be at least 21 years of age (18 for students with restrictions)
  • Possess a valid driver's license appropriate for the vehicle type
  • Complete the Driver Authorization Application
  • Consent to Motor Vehicle Record (MVR) check
  • Have an acceptable driving record (see 4.2)
  • Complete university driver safety training
  • Sign the Driver Responsibility Agreement

4.2 Acceptable Driving Record Standards

Violation Type (Past 3 Years) Maximum Allowed
Minor moving violations 3
At-fault accidents 2
Major violations (reckless driving, racing) 0
DUI/DWI convictions 0
License suspensions/revocations 0
Hit and run 0

Drivers not meeting these standards may be denied authorization or have authorization revoked.

5. Vehicle Reservation and Use

5.1 Reservation Process

  • Submit Vehicle Request Form at least 48 hours in advance
  • Include trip purpose, destination, dates, and number of passengers
  • Supervisor approval required
  • Transportation & Fleet confirms availability and assigns vehicle
  • Cancellations must be made at least 24 hours in advance

5.2 Vehicle Check-Out

  • Driver must present valid license at time of pickup
  • Complete pre-trip inspection with Fleet staff
  • Note any existing damage on inspection form
  • Receive keys, fuel card, and emergency information
  • Review vehicle operation if unfamiliar with vehicle

5.3 Vehicle Return

  • Return vehicle by scheduled time
  • Return with at least 1/4 tank of fuel (full tank preferred)
  • Complete post-trip inspection
  • Report any damage, mechanical issues, or incidents
  • Remove all personal belongings and trash
  • Return keys and fuel card to designated location

6. Driver Responsibilities

6.1 Safe Driving Requirements

  • Obey all traffic laws and posted speed limits
  • Wear seatbelts at all times; ensure all passengers wear seatbelts
  • Do not use cell phones while driving (hands-free allowed only if necessary)
  • No texting while operating a vehicle
  • Do not drive under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or medications that impair driving
  • Do not drive when fatigued
  • Adjust driving for weather and road conditions

6.2 Prohibited Activities

  • Personal use of university vehicles
  • Transporting unauthorized passengers
  • Transporting hitchhikers
  • Towing trailers without authorization
  • Smoking or vaping in university vehicles
  • Transporting hazardous materials (except as authorized)
  • Allowing unlicensed or unauthorized individuals to drive
  • Modifying vehicle equipment or removing safety devices

7. Accidents and Incidents

7.1 Immediate Response

  1. Stop immediately and ensure safety of all parties
  2. Call 911 if there are injuries or significant damage
  3. Do not admit fault or make statements about liability
  4. Exchange information with other parties (insurance, license, contact)
  5. Obtain names and contact information of witnesses
  6. Take photographs of damage and scene if possible
  7. Notify Campus Security and Transportation & Fleet

7.2 Reporting Requirements

  • Complete Accident Report Form within 24 hours
  • Submit police report number to Risk Management
  • All accidents are investigated regardless of severity
  • Post-accident drug/alcohol testing may be required

8. Personal Vehicles for University Business

8.1 Requirements

  • Submit Personal Vehicle Use Authorization Form annually
  • Maintain valid driver's license and registration
  • Maintain minimum liability insurance ($100,000/$300,000)
  • Driver's personal insurance is primary in case of accident
  • Mileage reimbursement per university Travel Policy

8.2 Limitations

  • University does not cover damage to personal vehicles
  • Comprehensive and collision coverage is owner's responsibility
  • Personal vehicles may not be used for transporting students unless specifically authorized

9. Student Drivers

9.1 Eligibility

  • Must be at least 18 years of age
  • Must have held license for at least 2 years
  • Must complete additional driver training
  • Faculty or staff advisor must approve

9.2 Restrictions

  • May only drive 12-passenger or smaller vehicles
  • May not drive 15-passenger vans
  • Night driving (11 PM - 6 AM) requires special approval
  • Long-distance trips require faculty/staff supervisor in vehicle

10. 15-Passenger Van Restrictions

Due to safety concerns with 15-passenger vans:

  • Maximum 10 passengers (including driver) unless approved driver certification obtained
  • No roof-top cargo carriers permitted
  • Additional training required for drivers
  • Students may not drive 15-passenger vans
  • Regular tire and maintenance inspections required

11. Violations and Consequences

Violations of this policy may result in:

  • Suspension or revocation of driving privileges
  • Responsibility for traffic citations and fines
  • Financial liability for damages due to negligence
  • Disciplinary action up to and including termination

12. Compliance and Review

This policy is reviewed annually by Transportation & Fleet Services and Risk Management. MVR checks are conducted annually for all authorized drivers. Driver training is required every three years.

13. SACSCOC Standards Referenced

  • Standard 13.7 (Physical Resources): The institution maintains physical resources, including vehicles, adequate to support its mission.
  • Standard 13.8 (Institutional Environment): The institution provides a safe and secure environment.
